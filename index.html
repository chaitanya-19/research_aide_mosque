MOSQUES IN ARIZONA

2010


1] Very first new article found on the internet about arizona mosques published by US news

2] The news titled “Mosque, Arizona Immigration Controversies Reflect Tough Economy” was published on oct 12 2010.The article connects economic hardship to racial intolerance in America, where people take out their frustrations on minorities, such as Muslims and Latinos, during tough times. The author mentions the controversy surrounding mosque construction near Ground Zero and similar protests across the country. The author also discusses recent court decisions on immigration and gay marriage and their impact on the march to equality, but acknowledges that some Americans are frustrated with their financial situation and feel ignored.

2011


1] One of the earliest mentions of muslims in phoenix came this year in form of a scholarly article “Muslim American/American Muslim Identity: Authoring Self in Post-9/11 America” which was published on oct 20 2011.

2] The article studies how Muslim American identity is formed in Phoenix, Arizona, through the experiences of immigrant and American-born Muslims. It analyzes the self-image and representations of Muslims in a discursive contact zone where America and Islam converge. Three themes emerge: Muslims see their Islam as "more authentic," fear their faith is under attack, and the meaning of the label "American Muslim/Muslim American" is not yet established.

2013


1] Two scholarly articles were published this year one of which talks about design of mosques for hot conditions of arizona.

2] Another article examines the perspectives of the younger generations of American-born/raised offspring of Muslim converts and immigrants on their roles in forming an American Muslim community and their views on identity, cultural Islam, and the Islamization of America. Using participant observation, interviews, and surveys, the study looks at how these individuals define themselves and their community, and how their faith affects their daily lives and future plans. The study takes place in the Phoenix and Chicago metropolitan areas.
